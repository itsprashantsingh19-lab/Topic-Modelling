06

Common Ground & Key Differences

Where do both leaders converge in their messaging? Where do they diverge most sharply?

🗳️
Electoral Rhetoric
Both leaders weight Electoral & Campaign Rhetoric similarly — Yogi at 26.7% and Owaisi at 24.9%. Both are deeply invested in the UP 2026 elections and use rallies, speeches and social media to mobilise voters.
📣
Anti-Opposition Framing
Both leaders spend significant discourse space attacking the opposing political camp — Yogi targets Congress/SP, Owaisi targets BJP. The structure of "us vs them" is universal across both.
📱
Social Media Presence
Both are prolific on X (Twitter). Yogi's handle @myogiadityanath and Owaisi's @asadowaisi are among the most-followed political accounts in India, with both using platforms for rapid issue framing.
🏗️
Yogi: Development Narrative
Yogi heavily focuses on Governance & Development (30.6%) — airports, expressways, investment summits. His discourse frames UP as an emerging economic powerhouse under BJP rule. Owaisi scores only 14.1% here.
⚖️
Owaisi: Rights Discourse
Owaisi's dominant theme is Social Justice & Rights at a striking 47.5% — nearly half his entire discourse. CAA/NRC opposition, police encounter accountability, and constitutional rights dominate. Yogi scores only 15.7% here.
🕉️
Yogi: Religious Identity
Yogi scores 27.0% on Religious & Identity Politics — Sanatan Dharma, Ram Mandir references, and temple visits dominate. Owaisi scores only 13.5% on this axis, and when he does engage religion it is defensively (Muslim rights).
📜
Owaisi: Constitutional Focus
Unlike Yogi who uses power of government office to speak, Owaisi operates primarily as a parliamentary opposition leader — invoking the Constitution, demanding accountability, and speaking through floor debates and press conferences.

Data Sources & Methodology

Articles were fetched via NewsAPI over 30 days, covering recent speeches, reports and coverage of both leaders.

📡
Data Collection

NewsAPI · 4 queries per leader · 126 articles total (91 Yogi, 35 Owaisi) · Sources: Indian Express, NDTV, Times of India, The Hindu, The Wire, ANI, PTI

🧹
Preprocessing

Lowercasing · URL removal · Custom stopword list (140+ terms) · Min word length filter · CountVectorizer (vocab: 612 terms, n-gram 1–2)

🔬
LDA Modelling

sklearn LDA · 4 topics · 25 iterations · batch learning · α=0.1 · β=0.01 · Perplexity ≈ 1314.5 (lower = better fit)
